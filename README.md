# India Union Budget Capability Analysis

## Overview

This project analyzes the Union Budget of India using the **Capability Approach**

The objective is to examine how public expenditure contributes to the expansion of human capabilities — the real freedoms people have to live healthy, educated, and economically secure lives.

By combining **economic theory, public policy analysis, and Python-based data analytics**, the project evaluates whether government spending prioritizes sectors that expand human capabilities.

---

## Research Motivation

Public budgets are one of the most powerful policy tools for shaping human development.

Traditional fiscal analysis often focuses on macroeconomic indicators such as deficits, growth, and fiscal balance. However, the **Capability Approach** shifts attention toward how public spending affects people's real opportunities and well-being.

This project asks a central policy question:

**To what extent does the Union Budget allocate resources toward sectors that expand human capabilities?**

---

## Analytical Framework

The analysis is grounded in the **Capability Approach**, which evaluates development in terms of people's ability to achieve valuable states of being and doing.

Government expenditure is interpreted through the lens of capability expansion across key domains:

* Education capability
* Health capability
* Nutrition and basic welfare
* Social protection
* Economic opportunity

Budget allocations are examined to understand how fiscal priorities shape the conditions necessary for human development.

---

## Data Sources

The analysis uses official Government of India budget documents, including:

* Budget at a Glance
* Demand for Grants
* Revenue Expenditure Statements

These documents provide detailed data on government spending across ministries, schemes, and sectors.

---

## Methodology

The project uses a data analytics pipeline implemented in Python:

1. Extraction of tables from official budget documents
2. Data cleaning and transformation
3. Classification of expenditure categories
4. Capability-based categorization of spending
5. Calculation of budget shares
6. Visualization of fiscal allocation patterns

The analysis is implemented in **Jupyter notebooks** to ensure transparency and reproducibility.
